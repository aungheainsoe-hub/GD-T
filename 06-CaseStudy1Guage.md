# Case Study 1: Functional Gauge Design

To inspect the bracket orientation and position constraints, a physical functional gauge is utilized. 
<img width="1081" height="766" alt="image" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/e315ad80-9c2a-46e8-bc55-5645eefa85bf" />
### Inspection Procedure
1. **Establish Orientation:** Secure the bracket flat against the primary datum simulator (the ground bottom face of the gauge block).
2. **Alignment & Verification:** Insert the hard gauge pins through the corresponding features simultaneously to verify part acceptance.
<img width="1347" height="817" alt="image" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/104a2a2e-e82d-4351-8652-3d003a19b4a6" />

### Gauge Pin Dimensioning Matrix
For internal features (holes and slots) controlled at MMC, the virtual condition boundary determines the physical gauge simulator size:
$$\text{Gauge Simulator Size} = \text{MMC Size} - \text{Geometric Tolerance}$$
<img width="1342" height="837" alt="image" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/0503868e-5d9c-4e60-97a4-a4c14c705c23" />
* **Hole A Pin Size:** $10.15\text{ mm}$ (MMC) $- 0.20\text{ mm}$ (GD&T) $= \mathbf{9.95\text{ mm}}$
* **Slot B Block Size:** $10.15\text{ mm}$ (MMC) $- 0.05\text{ mm}$ (GD&T) $= \mathbf{10.10\text{ mm}}$
* **Hole C Pin Size:** $6.20\text{ mm}$ (MMC) $- 0.20\text{ mm}$ (GD&T) $= \mathbf{6.00\text{ mm}}$

If all pins slide cleanly into the manufactured bracket while it rests against the base datum simulator, the part passes positional inspection instantly.
